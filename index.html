Problem Statement

Road accidents demand immediate medical response. Minutes matter—especially within the Golden Hour, when timely treatment significantly improves survival chances.

Why Road Accidents Are Critical

Accidents can cause severe trauma and internal injuries where early intervention is vital. Delays due to lack of awareness, poor communication, or traffic congestion lead to preventable fatalities.

  • Golden Hour: first ~60 minutes are crucial for emergency care.
  • Faster alerts: enables hospitals/police/ambulance to respond quickly.
  • Smart traffic: can create green corridors for ambulances.

Why Traditional Systems Fail

Conventional approaches often rely on manual reporting, which is slow and error-prone. Single-sensor triggers can cause false alarms (potholes, harsh braking), while real crashes may go unreported.

  • Manual calling delays emergency response.
  • False positives reduce system trust.
  • No multi-layer verification (impact + sound + vision).
  • Limited secure evidence storage for investigations.

Core Components

Sensors

Accelerometer: detects sudden impact / G-force spikes.

GPS: captures location for emergency services.

Microphone (optional): captures crash sounds for audio analysis.

ESP32

Collects sensor streams, performs lightweight validation, and transmits data via Wi‑Fi/Cellular modules to the cloud.

Cloud

Runs ML/CNN models, stores logs, triggers workflows (Firebase/IFTTT), and integrates with smart city services.

Alert System

Automatically sends accident alerts containing GPS location, time stamp, and confidence scores to emergency responders and nearby vehicles (V2X).

Multi-layer verification = fewer false alarms

The system confirms accidents using a combination of impact, motion, audio anomalies, and CNN confidence, rather than relying on a single trigger.

AI & Machine Learning

AI handles decision-making and orchestration, while ML learns driving/impact patterns from data.

Artificial Intelligence (AI)

AI acts as the system’s decision engine: it combines evidence from multiple sources (sensor thresholds, ML classification, CNN confidence) and decides whether to trigger emergency protocols.

  • Decision fusion: impact + sound + vision + context.
  • Triggers emergency workflow automatically.
  • Can integrate with smart traffic for green corridors.

Machine Learning (ML)

ML models analyze numeric sensor streams (accelerometer, speed changes, motion patterns) to distinguish normal events from true accidents.

  • Pattern detection from time-series signals.
  • Classifies: normal driving vs sudden braking vs accident.
  • Improves accuracy over time with new training data.

CNN (Convolutional Neural Networks)

CNNs learn spatial patterns in images and time-frequency patterns in spectrograms—ideal for accident detection.

What is a CNN?

A CNN is a deep learning model designed to extract features using convolution filters. It automatically learns edges, shapes, motion cues, and complex visual patterns.

Image-based Accident Detection

Road-facing camera frames can be classified into accident vs non-accident. This adds visual confirmation, improving reliability when sensors alone are ambiguous.

Camera→CNN→Confidence

Sound-based Detection (Spectrograms)

Crash sounds have distinctive frequency signatures. Audio is converted into a spectrogram and then classified by a CNN to detect collisions.

Mic→Spectrogram→CNN

Why combine CNN with sensors?

Sensors can spike during potholes or harsh braking. CNN-based vision/audio adds an independent signal, enabling robust multi-layer validation and reducing false positives.

Conclusion & Future Scope

AI-enabled safety systems can transform emergency response, reduce fatalities, and power smart transportation.

Key Benefits

  • Reduced response time through automated emergency alerts.
  • Higher accuracy via multi-layer verification (sensor + CNN + ML).
  • Better traffic coordination through V2X / smart city integration.
  • Secure incident records using blockchain-based logging.

Future Scope

  • Edge-AI deployment for low-latency on-device inference.
  • Expanded health monitoring and driver fatigue prediction.
  • City-wide integration with adaptive traffic signals and routing.
  • Continuous learning using anonymized, consent-based datasets.
